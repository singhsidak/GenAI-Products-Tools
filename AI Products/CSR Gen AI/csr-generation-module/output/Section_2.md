**Summary of Results:**

**Efficacy Results:**

In the final analysis of the primary efficacy endpoint among participants without prior evidence of SARS-CoV-2 infection, vaccine efficacy (VE) against confirmed COVID-19 occurring at least 7 days after Dose 2 was 95.0% (95% credible interval: 90.3% to 97.6%). There were 8 cases of COVID-19 in the BNT162b2 group (N=18,242) compared to 162 cases in the placebo group (N=18,379).

For the second primary endpoint, which included participants with and without evidence of prior SARS-CoV-2 infection, VE against confirmed COVID-19 occurring at least 7 days after Dose 2 was 94.6% (95% credible interval: 89.9% to 97.3%), with 9 cases in the BNT162b2 group and 169 in the placebo group.

A total of 10 cases of severe COVID-19 were observed after Dose 1, with 1 case in the BNT162b2 group and 9 cases in the placebo group.

**Safety Results:**

BNT162b2 was well-tolerated. In the safety population of participants aged ≥16 years (N=43,252), the number of participants reporting at least one AE was higher in the BNT162b2 group (26.7%) compared to the placebo group (12.2%). This was largely driven by transient, mild-to-moderate reactogenicity events.

The most common adverse reactions were injection site pain (>80%), fatigue (>60%), headache (>50%), myalgia (>40%), chills (>30%), arthralgia (>20%), and fever (>10%). The incidence of serious adverse events was low and comparable between the BNT162b2 group (0.6%) and the placebo group (0.5%). Six deaths were reported (2 in the BNT162b2 group, 4 in the placebo group); none were considered by the investigator to be related to the study intervention.

**Conclusion:**

A two-dose regimen of BNT162b2 was highly effective in preventing COVID-19 and demonstrated a favorable safety profile in participants aged 16 years and older. The benefits of the vaccine in preventing COVID-19 outweigh the known and potential risks.
