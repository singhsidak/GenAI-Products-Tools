# 8. Study Objectives

The overall purpose of this Phase 1/2/3 study was to evaluate the safety, tolerability, immunogenicity, and efficacy of RNA vaccine candidates against COVID-19 in healthy individuals. The study was divided into two main parts: a Phase 1 dose-finding and vaccine candidate-selection stage, and a Phase 2/3 expanded cohort and efficacy stage. The specific objectives for each phase are detailed below.

# 8.1 Phase 1 Objectives

The primary objective of the Phase 1 portion of the study was to identify the preferred vaccine candidate(s) and dose level(s). Key objectives are summarized in the table below.

Table 8.1: Phase 1 Study Objectives
| Category | Objective |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Safety and Tolerability** | To describe the safety and tolerability profiles of different vaccine candidates and dose levels after vaccination. |
| **Immunogenicity** | To describe the immune response elicited by different vaccine candidates and dose levels after vaccination. |

# 8.2 Phase 2/3 Objectives

# 8.2.1 Primary Objectives

The primary objectives for the Phase 2/3 portion of the study focused on evaluating the efficacy and safety of the selected vaccine candidate, BNT162b2.

Table 8.2: Phase 2/3 Primary Objectives
| Category | Objective |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Efficacy** | To evaluate the efficacy of BNT162b2 against confirmed COVID-19 occurring from 7 days after the second dose in participants without evidence of infection before vaccination. |
| **Efficacy** | To evaluate the efficacy of BNT162b2 against confirmed COVID-19 occurring from 7 days after the second dose in participants with and without evidence of infection before vaccination. |
| **Safety** | To define the safety profile of BNT162b2 in all participants randomized in Phase 2/3. |
| **Safety** | To define the safety profile of BNT162b2 in participants 12 to 15 years of age in Phase 3. |

# 8.2.2 Secondary Objectives

The secondary objectives for the Phase 2/3 portion of the study were designed to further characterize the efficacy and immunogenicity of BNT162b2.

Table 8.3: Phase 2/3 Secondary Objectives
| Category | Objective |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Efficacy** | To evaluate the efficacy of BNT162b2 against confirmed COVID-19 occurring from 14 days after the second dose in participants without evidence of infection before vaccination. |
| **Efficacy** | To evaluate the efficacy of BNT162b2 against confirmed COVID-19 occurring from 14 days after the second dose in participants with and without evidence of infection before vaccination. |
| **Efficacy** | To evaluate the efficacy of BNT162b2 against confirmed severe COVID-19 occurring from 7 days and from 14 days after the second dose in participants without evidence of infection before vaccination. |
| **Efficacy** | To evaluate the efficacy of BNT162b2 against confirmed severe COVID-19 occurring from 7 days and from 14 days after the second dose in participants with and without evidence of infection before vaccination. |
| **Efficacy** | To describe the efficacy of BNT162b2 against confirmed COVID-19 (according to the CDC-defined symptoms) occurring from 7 days and from 14 days after the second dose in participants without evidence of infection before vaccination. |
| **Efficacy** | To describe the efficacy of BNT162b2 against confirmed COVID-19 (according to the CDC-defined symptoms) occurring from 7 days and from 14 days after the second dose in participants with and without evidence of infection before vaccination. |
| **Immunogenicity** | To demonstrate the noninferiority of the immune response to BNT162b2 in participants 12 to 15 years of age compared to participants 16 to 25 years of age. |

# 8.2.3 Exploratory Objectives

Exploratory objectives were included to investigate additional aspects of the immune response and the vaccine's performance in specific populations.

Table 8.4: Phase 2/3 Exploratory Objectives
| Category | Objective |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Immunogenicity** | To evaluate the immune response over time to BNT162b2 and persistence of immune response in participants with and without serological or virological evidence of SARS-CoV-2 infection before vaccination. |
| **Immunogenicity** | To evaluate the immune response (non-S) to SARS-CoV-2 in participants with and without confirmed COVID-19 during the study. |
| **Immunogenicity** | To describe the serological responses to the BNT vaccine candidate in cases of: confirmed COVID-19, confirmed severe COVID-19, and SARS-CoV-2 infection without confirmed COVID-19. |
| **Special Population** | To describe the safety, immunogenicity, and efficacy of BNT162b2 in individuals with confirmed stable HIV disease. |
| **Manufacturing** | To describe the safety and immunogenicity of BNT162b2 in individuals 16 to 55 years of age vaccinated with study intervention produced by two different manufacturing processes. |
