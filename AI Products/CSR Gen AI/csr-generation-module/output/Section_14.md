# 14.3.3 Listings of Deaths

There were 6 participants in the Phase 2/3 study who died through the data cutoff date of 14 November 2020. This included 2 participants in the BNT162b2 group and 4 participants in the placebo group. None of these deaths were assessed by the investigator as related to study intervention.

Details of the 6 reported deaths are as follows:

-   One participant in the older BNT162b2 group experienced an SAE of arteriosclerosis and died 3 days after Dose 1.
-   One participant in the older BNT162b2 group experienced an SAE of cardiac arrest 60 days after Dose 2 and died 3 days later.
-   One participant in the younger placebo group experienced an SAE of unevaluable event (unknown of unknown origin; no additional information currently available at the time of this report) 8 days after Dose 1 and died the same day.
-   One participant in the older placebo group experienced an SAE of hemorrhagic stroke 15 days after Dose 2 and died the next day.
-   One participant in the younger placebo group experienced an SAE of death (cause unknown; no additional information currently available at the time of this report) 34 days after Dose 2.
-   One participant in the older placebo group experienced an SAE of myocardial infarction 16 days after Dose 1 and died the same day.

An additional event of death due to aortic rupture was reported after the cut-off date and up to 5-Dec-20 (data blinded).

# 14.3.4 Narratives of Other Serious and Significant Adverse Events

Beyond the serious adverse events leading to death, several other serious adverse events were reported during the study.

Three serious adverse events (SAEs) in the BNT162b2 group were assessed by the investigator as related to study intervention:
-   One SAE of shoulder injury related to vaccine administration.
-   One SAE of ventricular arrhythmia.
-   One SAE of lymphadenopathy.
Additionally, one SAE of lower back pain and bilateral lower extremity pain with radicular paresthesia was assessed by the investigator as related to study intervention in the BNT162b2 younger age group, with onset 47 days after Dose 2.

A total of 12 participants experienced SAEs of appendicitis (8 in the BNT162b2 group and 4 in the placebo group). This included appendicitis (7), appendicitis perforated (1) in the BNT162b2 group, and appendicitis (2), appendicitis perforated (1), complicated appendicitis (1) in the placebo group. None of these appendicitis cases were assessed by the investigators as related to study intervention. Of the 8 total appendicitis cases in the BNT162b2 group, 6 occurred in the younger age group and 2 occurred in the older age group.

In participants 16 to 17 years of age, 1 participant in the BNT162b2 group experienced an SAE of facial bones fracture, which was not considered related to study intervention by the investigator.

Adverse events of clinical interest were also reviewed. Lymphadenopathy was reported in 64 participants (0.3%) in the BNT162b2 group (54 [0.5%] in the younger age group and 10 [0.1%] in the older age group), compared to 6 participants in the placebo group. Most lymphadenopathy events were reported within 2 to 4 days after vaccination.
