# 10.2 Patients Screened and Reasons for Exclusion

Specific numerical data regarding the total number of patients screened for inclusion and the precise breakdown of reasons for excluding patients during screening are not available in the provided documentation. However, the study protocol outlined clear inclusion and exclusion criteria to define the appropriate patient population.

**Key Exclusion Criteria included:**
- Medical or psychiatric conditions increasing study risk or making participation inappropriate (e.g., active suicidal ideation/behavior, laboratory abnormality).
- Known infection with human immunodeficiency virus (HIV), hepatitis C virus (HCV), or hepatitis B virus (HBV) (for Phases 1 and 2 only).
- History of severe adverse reaction to a vaccine or severe allergic reaction to any study intervention component.
- Receipt of medications intended to prevent COVID-19.
- Previous clinical or microbiological diagnosis of COVID-19.
- Individuals at high risk for severe COVID-19 (e.g., hypertension, diabetes, chronic pulmonary disease, asthma, obesity, anticipating immunosuppressive treatment) (Phase 1 only).
- Immunocompromised individuals.
- History of autoimmune disease or active autoimmune disease requiring therapeutic intervention (Phase 1 only).
- Bleeding diathesis or condition contraindicating intramuscular injection.
- Women who are pregnant or breastfeeding.
- Previous vaccination with any coronavirus vaccine.
- Receipt of immunosuppressive therapy (e.g., cytotoxic agents, systemic corticosteroids) or planned receipt throughout the study.
- Receipt of blood/plasma products or immunoglobulin within 60 days before study intervention.
- Participation in other studies involving study intervention within 28 days prior to study entry or during participation.
- Previous participation in other studies involving lipid nanoparticles.
- Positive serological test for SARS-CoV-2 IgM and/or IgG antibodies at screening (Phase 1 only).
- Any screening hematology and/or blood chemistry laboratory value ≥ Grade 1 abnormality (Phase 1 only, with exceptions for stable Grade 1 abnormalities).
- Positive test for HIV, HBsAg, HBc Abs, or HCV Abs at screening (Phase 1 only).
- SARS-CoV-2 NAAT-positive nasal swab within 24 hours before study intervention (Phase 1 only).
- Investigator site staff or sponsor employees directly involved in the study and their family members.

# 10.3 Post-Randomisation Discontinuations

The primary reasons for post-randomization discontinuation of study vaccine by participants included no longer meeting eligibility criteria (e.g., due to a previous clinical or microbiological diagnosis of COVID-19), withdrawal by the participant, and adverse events (AEs). Discontinuations due to AEs were reported for approximately 0.1% of participants in both the BNT162b2 and placebo groups. The overall reasons for definitive discontinuation of study intervention, as per the protocol, could also include investigator request, pregnancy, or protocol deviation.

It is important to note that discontinuation of study intervention did not necessarily represent withdrawal from the study. The study design mandated that if study intervention was definitively discontinued, the participant would remain in the study for evaluation of safety, immunogenicity, and efficacy. The provided documentation does not explicitly state whether the blind for individual patients was broken at the time of discontinuation of study intervention or withdrawal from the study.

# 10.4 Follow-up Duration

Participants who discontinued study intervention remained in the study to be evaluated for safety, immunogenicity, and efficacy. They continued to adhere to the normal visit schedule. Specifically, participants were to be followed for non-serious AEs for 1 month and serious AEs for 6 months after the second dose of BNT162b2. This follow-up included unscheduled telephone contacts and/or in-person visits as necessary to elicit AEs. The only exception to continued follow-up was if a participant specifically withdrew consent for any further contact or information collection.

# 10.5 Protocol Deviations

Important protocol deviations identified during the study primarily related to vaccine administration and eligibility. While a detailed summary by center is not available, the following categories of deviations were observed:

- **Dosing/Administration Errors:** There was an imbalance in exclusions from the evaluable efficacy population due to dosing/administration errors. Specifically, 105 participants in the BNT162b2 group and 3 in the placebo group were excluded because they did not receive the correct dose of vaccine. One isolated dosing/administrative error event in a single clinical center affected 52 participants receiving BNT162b2. Additionally, 2 participants received an extra dose of BNT162b2 (30 μg) at an unscheduled visit after receiving one dose of BNT162b2 (30 μg) and one dose of placebo.
- **Investigational Product (IP) Suitability:** A significant number of participants (144 in the BNT162b2 group and 0 in the placebo group) were excluded because the investigational product administered was deemed not suitable for use by Almac. This imbalance was attributed to the differing storage conditions required for BNT162b2 (ultracold) compared to placebo (ambient), leading to temperature excursions being an issue predominantly for BNT162b2.
- **Eligibility Criteria:** Some participants discontinued study vaccine because they no longer met eligibility criteria, with the most common reason being a previous clinical or microbiological diagnosis of COVID-19.
