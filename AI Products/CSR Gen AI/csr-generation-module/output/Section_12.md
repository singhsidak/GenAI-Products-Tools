# 12. Safety Evaluation

# 12.1 Extent of Exposure

The safety population for this study included all randomized subjects aged 12 through 15 years who received at least one dose of the investigational product (BNT162b2) or placebo. The extent of exposure to the investigational product and placebo was characterised by the number of subjects exposed, the duration of exposure, and the dose administered.

A total of 2264 subjects aged 12 through 15 years were randomized in the study, with 1134 subjects randomized to the BNT162b2 group and 1130 to the placebo group. The safety analysis included 1131 subjects in the BNT162b2 group and 1129 subjects in the placebo group who received at least one dose.

The investigational product administered was BNT162b2 at a dose of 30 µg. Dosing was based on absolute amount and not on a mg/kg or mg/m² basis.

# 12.2.5 Assessment of Significance

The overall safety profile of BNT162b2 in subjects aged 12 to 15 years was generally favorable during the blinded, placebo-controlled follow-up period. The incidence of serious adverse events (SAEs) was low and balanced between the BNT162b2 and placebo groups in the broader study population. In the subset of 12-15 year olds, no SAEs were reported. No deaths were considered related to the study intervention.

The investigator's assessment of causality for SAEs indicated a limited number of events considered related to BNT162b2 in the overall study population (4 events in BNT162b2 vs 0 in placebo). These included lymphadenopathy and shoulder injury, which are generally manageable and consistent with vaccine reactogenicity profiles. No previously unsuspected important adverse effects of the investigational product were identified in this age group.

Further details regarding deaths, other serious adverse events, and other significant adverse events leading to withdrawal, dose reduction, or institution of concomitant therapy, along with narrative statements, are presented in Section 14.3.2 and 14.3.3, respectively. The significance of these events is assessed with respect to the safety of BNT162b2. No life table or similar analyses for SAEs were identified in the provided documents specifically for this age group or related to time on investigational product.

# 12.5 Safety Conclusions

The overall safety evaluation of BNT162b2 in subjects aged 12 to 15 years demonstrated a generally favorable safety profile. The extent of exposure was adequate to assess safety, with a large number of subjects receiving two doses of the 30 µg vaccine or placebo. The duration of follow-up for the primary series was sufficient to identify common and early-onset adverse events.

Adverse events observed were generally mild to moderate in severity, and the incidence of most AEs was comparable between the vaccine and placebo groups, with some expected reactogenicity more common in the vaccine group. Serious adverse events were infrequent and balanced between treatment groups in the larger study population, with no SAEs reported in the subset of 12-15 year olds. No deaths were attributed to the investigational product.

No specific patients or patient groups were identified to be at increased risk within the 12-15 year age cohort based on the available data, and no particular vulnerabilities were noted for this population, considering their general healthy status.

The safety evaluation supports the possible use of BNT162b2 in the 12 to 15 year age group, with an adverse event profile that is generally consistent with other vaccines. Ongoing long-term follow-up studies will continue to monitor the safety profile of the vaccine.## 12.5 Safety Conclusions
