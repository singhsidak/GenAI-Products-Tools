# 5. Ethics

# 5.1. Independent Ethics Committee (IEC) or Institutional Review Board (IRB)

The study protocol, any amendments, the Informed Consent Document (ICD), and other relevant study-related documents were reviewed and approved by an Independent Ethics Committee (IEC) or Institutional Review Board (IRB) at each participating study site prior to the initiation of the study.

Any subsequent amendments to the protocol also required IEC/IRB approval before the implementation of the changes to the study design, except for changes necessary to eliminate an immediate hazard to study participants. A comprehensive list of all IECs and IRBs consulted is provided in Appendix 16.1.3.

# 5.2. Ethical Conduct of the Study

The study was conducted in accordance with the consensus ethical principles derived from international guidelines, including the Declaration of Helsinki and the Council for International Organizations of Medical Sciences (CIOMS) International Ethical Guidelines. The study also adhered to the applicable International Council for Harmonisation (ICH) Good Clinical Practice (GCP) guidelines and all applicable local laws and regulations.

# 5.3. Patient Information and Consent

Written informed consent was obtained from each participant, or their legally acceptable representative, before any study-specific procedures were performed. The investigator or a designated representative provided a comprehensive explanation of the nature, objectives, and potential risks of the study to each potential participant. Sufficient time was provided for participants to ask questions and make a voluntary decision about their participation.

The process ensured that each participant was fully informed about the nature of the study, the sharing of study-related data, and their right to withdraw from the study at any time without prejudice. A signed and dated copy of the Informed Consent Document was provided to each participant. The medical records for each participant document that written informed consent was obtained prior to their enrolment in the study.

A representative sample of the written information provided to participants and the Informed Consent Document can be found in Appendix 16.1.3.
